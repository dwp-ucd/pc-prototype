V15 / Index V1

    Start prototype Skype test 03.04.2020

  • Version notes and shortcuts
    Introduction: Design iterations based on feed back of UR to this date / (Skype, X.0X.2020)

    Design development and UR outcomes:

    • Service matching: Find a person
    • Immediate functional value of citizen information via APIs.
    • Journey: How does the APIs effect the Pension Credit service
    • User research and usability findings and outcomes

    Changes, About the testing & What we tested:

    • NI router: NN 11 22 33 P = Refined benefits (Name only)
    • NI router: NN 11 22 33 M = Details don't match
    • NI router: NN 11 22 33 Y = Too young
    • NI router: NN 11 22 33 F = 500 error / broken
    • NI router: NN 11 22 33 B = Benefits (All data)
    • NI router: NN 11 22 33 R = Claimant is getting PC
    • NI router: NN 11 22 33 T = Claimant is getting PC via a partner
    • Changed: How to move forward when API has failed.

    Assumptions tested:

    • XXXXX

    ShortCuts:

    • Claim-filter
    • Start
    • Task list

    Outcome:

    • Both advisers felt that this solution would prevent questions getting missed on the call.
    • ‘Do you want to apply for support with interest’ We don’t take applications for this and we don’t believe an application form gets sent out. Instead what is sent is an information pack. “We will send you more information by post including details about how to apply”. Advisers felt this was the best position to ask this question.
    • Main concern with having two PDFs (one for PC one for HB) was if an adviser forgot to download the ‘Housing Benefit PDF’ meaning they’ve lost of the data for the application.
    • Advisers weren’t at first sure what the call to action ‘End call and download claim’ was going to do. 1 adviser thought this might have downloaded the claim, the other thought it could have ended the call. “Can it can not just say next as it’s not finished.”
    • Advisers tell people what to expect after they complete a Housing Benefit application, they mention that the details will be passed to their Local Authority, they also tell the person to go to their LA (it was mixed whether they needed the entitlement notice first). There was a lack of understanding what a LA does with the partial Housing Benefit claim and whether they do anything at all. Adviser didn’t think this got covered in the entitlement notice.
    • 1 adviser was concerned that we’d be ending the call before downloading the PDF, meaning that if the download didn’t work the data would be lost. They mentioned that they’ve not had a problem with the download not working. “Shouldn’t be letting them go until we have the proof and the data.”
    • The question ‘Do you spend over 35 hours caring for someone isn’t detailed enough, the person they’re caring for has to be on AA, condensed AA, DLA, or PIP.’
    • Adviser also mentioned the content ‘You might be able to get some help from your local authority’ is inaccurate as Carers Allowance is a benefit DWP providers, they also weren’t sure you could apply online for this. You can apply for this over the phone or we can send a paper application form out.

V9
End-to-end testing fake call Warrington

    Start prototype 12.9.2019

  • Version notes and shortcuts
    Introduction: Agent testing, full end-to-end / Warrington / 12 September 2019

    About the testing:

    • We tested the prototype as a fake call with a scenario that aimed to test adding hospital admission details, income over 10k, employment details and the agent instruction screens.

    Assumptions tested:

    • Do agents understand what the agent instruction pages are telling them to do.
    • Do agents understand how to go back and change information.
    • Do agents understand how to add employment and where to add it.
    • Do agents understand how to add hospital admissions.
    • Do agents know whether a caller would have over or under £10,000.

    Changes made for testing:

    • Hospital admissions
    • Pension overview
    • Benefits overview
    • Earnings
    • Final CYA (PC summary)

    What we tested

    • Prototype.
    • Printed agent instruction screens.

    Outcome

    • Wasn’t always clear what to ask and what not to ask.
      Some of the agent instructions were read out as questions and some questions skipped
    • Agents didn’t know if the totals given were over or under 10,000k. 1 agent asked this as a question the other 3 selected ‘no’ despite the amount being over 10k because they weren’t sure
    • Agents were concerned about inputting text into a text box. It was noted as being slow, relying on spelling and could mean not enough information gets collected
    • Agents didn’t know when to check / ask about State Pension, it wasn’t clear what section this belonged to. Agents weren’t sure whether the instruction of check Searchlight for Pensions included ‘State Pension’
    • Wasn’t clear when to discuss what date they want to claim. 2 agents ending up getting to the ‘reason for date’ screen before they’d mentioned that date the user could claim from.
    • Where do I add Pensions and Benefits? Agents were expecting somewhere to add in Pensions and benefits.
    • Adding money, savings and investment is slow. There was comments that there was a lot of clicking around and took a while to get through compared to what they do now.
    • Indepth findings for this testing

    • What are agents having to write down?

    ShortCuts:

    Outcome:

    • CAM still timing out after 15 minutes due to new ‘tool bar’.
    • Still can’t process a case for 30 odd days.
    • A couple of the agents we tested with the prototype ran really slow, they commented that this wasn’t unusual.
    • It differed how much information they collected from G4S, the 3 ‘newer’ members of staff asked for more information that the agents who had been there for over 10 years.
    • Agents write information about the claimant on a paper pad or the computer notepad, they write down information they get from G4S plus what they see in searchlight. Security > Check information > Start claim.
    • Agents told us they do security and then confirm that all the information before we have is correct such as telephone number and address, this is also the point they ask if they have a mobile number. They do all of this ‘before starting the claim’.

    • There was a lot agents said they wouldn’t read out, including parts of the declaration and privacy policy. They tend to keep it short as people stop listening and just say ‘yeah, yeah’.
    • There were some comments about the speed of moving through, 1 agent said there’s a lot of ‘clicking’ another said that some questions felt spread out eg asking about address twice and this could ‘confuse people’.
    • Indepth findings for this testing

V8
Fake call feedback SME Debbie

    Start Prototype / End-2-end 15.7.2019

  • Version notes and shortcuts
    Introduction: Testing with Debby our new SME / Benton Park View / 15 July 2019

    Changes, About the testing & What we tested:


    • MSIC / Rolling total
    • MSIC / Last four digits
    • MSIC / Evidence reminder: Added in a checkbox for agents who beleive the data they are receiving from a user is wooly

    Assumptions tested (Tested 3 fake claim scenarios with a new claims SME): We wanted to understand how the full flow worked with a New Claims agent.
    We have been struggling to get in front of new claims agents, recently we have only had access to Changes agents.

    • Date of Claim: New way to collect what date the claim needs to be taken from
    • MSIC / New rolling summary
    • MSIC / Hint text on accounts
    • MSIC / New start of MSIC, where you Pension Credit to be paid

    Outcome:

    • Need to ensure we are collecting information from the right period
    • Information in the declaration isn’t the right position for some of the information, eg change of circs
    • It wasn’t clear enough what ‘changes in your circumstance’ mean in regards to the backdating.
    • It wasn’t clear what information had to go in screens where there was a open text field (Other people in your home, Undeclared pensions).
    • Concerns that people might not understand what is meant by ‘UK national’.
    • It was raised that by manually entering date of claim there could be a risk of the wrong date could be captured meaning that the information that gets collected after the claim is wrong.
    • The agent we spoke to was unfamiliar with all of the contact preference options, some of the options they’d never heard of eg local service contact.
    • It was pointed out that there is no way to distinguish between two similar accounts, at the moment they collect the last 4 digits on similar accounts to be able to distinguish between the two.
    • It was mentioned that we need to know if someone else is living in a second property.
    • It wasn’t clear whether the account that the PC was going to be paid into needed to be added again when going through M,S and I.

    Next steps:

    • Revisit position of the content in the declaration
    • Add examples of what could be classed as a ‘change of circumstance’.
    • Remove open text fields.
    • Understand more if we need to ask if they have a POA or if there is other ways of identifying this.
    • Design ‘change’ routes.
    • Revisit ‘date of claim’ journey.
    • Investigate contact preferences options.
    • Investigate what we need to ask for cash lump sums.
    • Make it clearer whether the account that the PC is to paid into has been added.

A.1.0

    Start Prototype (New claim / Full gather, no feeds) Tested

  • Version notes and shortcuts
    Introduction:
    Explored alternative ordering of questions attempting to eliminate any questions that were not needed for a claim. Questions were asked in a completely linear fashion (one questions per page.) We designed with the assumption that this would work for both agents and citizens.

    Tested in Dundee 8.10.2018 / Five participants
    About the testing & What we tested:

    • How would this work as a linear journey?
    • Does the order of the questions make sense?
    • What questions can we remove?
    • What other tools would an agent need to use to process a claim?

    Outcomes:

    • Agents were able to navigate through the prototype, although 2 thought they’d reached the end a step early due the label of the CTA on the page.
    • Agents commented that the order of the questions around where the user lives didn’t make sense as they weren’t all grouped together.
    • Agents mentioned being able to use a lot of other tools to double check information users provided, searchlight, CIS, RTI.
    • Agents were concerned users might not understand all the information we need, particularly financial.

    Next steps:

    • Investigate how we could use data from other systems, instead of having to ask the user for the information.
    • Test the questions with users to see what they do / don’t understand.
    • Test alternative ways of grouping the questions.

    Tested in Blaydon job centre 18.12.2018
    About the testing & What we tested:

    • What are users expectations of how they apply for Pension Credit and the process?
    • Would these users be more likely to use a digital service?
    • Test users understanding of the questions we are asking.
    • Understand what information users would know vs not know (What would they have to go away and check).

    Outcomes

    • 3 / 5 users not comfortable using the digital service to apply for Pension Credit, this was mostly due to the length of the form and the fear of completing it incorrectly.
    • Users liked being able to see the information in front of them, which was something they didn’t like about the applying on the phone.
    • Some of the terminology confused users, household composition, state, P60.
    • 3 user answered the question ‘Have you lived outside the UK in the last 2 years’ incorrectly, ticking ‘yes’ when they should have selected ‘No’.
    • The question of ‘when do you want to claim’ confused users, they were unsure when they could claim from.
    • 2 user did not realise the next step was to click on the text links, insteading expecting to click on the ‘continue’ button at the bottom of the page.
    • 2 users expressed concern after reading the declaration. Worrying that if they’d made a mistake they might be prosecuted.

    Next steps

    • Revisit wording on residency ‘Q’
    • Reword content such as ‘Household composition’
    • Add some content / guidance to help users understand the date they need to enter.
    • Test with citizens and their ‘helpers’ as a lot of the users have said they’d normally have seeked help with this type of application
