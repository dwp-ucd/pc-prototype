Who gets the State Pension?

You can claim your State Pension when you reach State Pension age.

This guide is also available in Welsh (Cymraeg).

If you reached State Pension age before 6 April 2016 you will get the basic State Pension. If the date you reach State Pension age is on or after 6 April 2016, you will get the new State pension.

Minimum number of qualifying years you need

To get any new State Pension (the date you reach State Pension age is on or after 6 April 2016) you need 10 qualifying years on your National Insurance record.

To get any basic State Pension (the date you reached State Pension age was before 6 April 2016) you need:

  • 11 qualifying years if you’re a man
  • 10 qualifying years if you’re a woman
  • 1 qualifying year if you’re a man born between 1945 and 1951
  • 1 qualifying years if you’re a woman born between 1950 and 1953

Your National Insurance qualifying years

National Insurance qualifying years are years in which you have:

  • worked and paid National Insurance
  • got National Insurance Credits, for example you were unemployed, sick, or a parent or carer
  • paid voluntary National Insurance contributions

Any years in which you paid married women’s or widow’s reduced rate contributions. might also be qualifying years.

The number of qualifying years on your National Insurance record affects how much State Pension you get. If you have not yet reached State Pension age, you can check your State Pension forecast to see what you might get.

If you’re married or in a civil partnership

You might be able to increase or inherit State Pension if you have had a spouse or civil partner .

If you’re transgender

Your State Pension might be affected if you’re a transgender person and you:

  • were born between 24 December 1919 and 3 April 1945
  • claimed State Pension before 4 April 2005
  • can provide evidence that your gender reassignment surgery took place before 4 April 2005

Find out more and contact the Gender Recognition team.

You do not need to do anything if you legally changed your gender and started claiming State Pension on or after 4 April 2005 - you’ll already be claiming based on your legal gender.

Help understanding your eligibility

To get information about your basic State Pension eligibility, contact the Pension Service or the International Pension Centre if you live abroad.
