Money received as a lump sum

Pension lump sums are taken into account for PC, but they are included in the amount for whatever account they are paid into.

We could consider an field in NSP to record the account it was paid into (last 4 digits, bank name etc.) The fact that they had a lump sum is recorded in ISCS/PTP CAM currently but the only purpose it serves is to show that they had one.

Personal Injury payments must have been made in consequence of a personal injury to the customer or partner. The lump sum payment and any regular payment made as an annuity from the claim are disregarded

A brief summary includes:

  • Vaccine damage payments
  • lump sum awards of damages
  • out of court settlements (insurance companies etc.)
  • lump sum gratuities under the War Pensions and Industrial Injuries Schemes
  • Criminal Injury Compensation Scheme payments
  • Lump sum payments made to people injured in an accident or disaster following charitable collections or public donations
  • Lump sum payments from accident insurance
