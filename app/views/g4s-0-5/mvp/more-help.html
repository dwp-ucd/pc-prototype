Other help with your living or housing costs

There is other help you could get, such as help with:

  • your health
  • heating your home
  • public transport
  • paying rent for your home
  • your pensions
  • benefits in general

Would you like to find out more about any of those?

Help with your health

Attendance Allowance helps with extra costs if you have a disability severe enough that you need someone to help look after you.

Go to GOV.UK and search for "attendance allowance".

Or call 0800 731 0122 (Monday to Friday, 9:30am-3:30pm).

Help with heating your home

If you were born on or before 5 October 1954 you could get between £100 and £300 to help you pay your heating bills. This is known as a Winter Fuel Payment.

Go to GOV.UK and search for "winter fuel payment".

Or call 0800 731 0160 (Monday to Friday, 9:30am-3:30pm).

Help with public transport

In England you can get a bus pass for free travel when you reach the female State Pension age, whether you are a man or a woman.

Go to GOV.UK and search for "free bus pass".

Or call your local council as they issue the bus passes.

Help with renting your house or flat

Housing Benefit can help you pay your rent if you are unemployed, on a low income or claiming benefits. It is being replaced by Universal Credit.

Go to GOV.UK and search for "housing benefit".

Or call your local council as they will deal with your claim.

Help with your pensions

The Pensions Advisory Service offers free information and advice about your State Pension or your company, personal, stakeholder or occupational pensions.

Go to GOV.UK and search for "TPAS".

Or call 0800 011 3797 (Monday to Friday, 9am-5pm).

General help with benefits

Go to GOV.UK and search for "benefits checker".

You will find links to a number of independent benefits calculators to help you find out what benefits you could get, how to claim and how your benefits will be affected if you start work.
