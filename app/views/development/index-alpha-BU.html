    • B.1.0

    • Start Prototype (Only ask what we don't know)
       Part tested

    • Version notes and module order and shortcuts

      Discription:
      This design explored if we could only ask the user questions we couldn’t source elsewhere, which we found was 6 questions. The rest of the data would be after call work completed by an agent.

      Our assumption was this process would reduce the amount of questions the user has to answer, and improve accuracy by not relying on the user providing the information.

      What we tested: Tested 19 November 2018 with Nick Valentine and David Armstrong. Internal Digital team, Longbenton. Tested approch of small under 10 question approch (Nick data collecton) and passed to David to process the claim using an A14 form.

      Outcome: This revealed what claims we were able to process and what was the outstanding information. The following steps would be to then make contact with the claimant, veryfiy and collect missing information.

    • A.1.0

    • Start Prototype (New claim / Full gather, no feeds)
       Tested

    • Version notes and module order and shortcuts

      Discription:
      Explored alternative ordering of questions attempting to eliminate any questions that were not needed for a claim. Questions were asked in a completely linear fashion (one questions per page.) We designed with the assumption that this would work for both agents and citizens.

      Tested in Dundee 8.10.2018 / Five participants.

      What we tested:
      How would this work as a linear journey?
      Does the order of the questions make sense?
      What questions can we remove?
      What other tools would an agent need to use to process a claim?

      Outcome:
      Agents were able to navigate through the prototype, although 2 thought they’d reached the end a step early due the label of the CTA on the page.
      Agents commented that the order of the questions around where the user lives didn’t make sense as they weren’t all grouped together.
      Agents mentioned being able to use a lot of other tools to double check information users provided, searchlight, CIS, RTI.
      Agents were concerned users might not understand all the information we need, particularly financial.

      Next steps:
      Investigate how we could use data from other systems, instead of having to ask the user for the information.
      Test the questions with users to see what they do / don’t understand.
      Test alternative ways of grouping the questions.



      Tested in Blaydon job centre 18.12.2018

      What we tested:
      What are users expectations of how they apply for Pension Credit and the process?
      Would these users be more likely to use a digital service?
      Test users understanding of the questions we are asking.
      Understand what information users would know vs not know (What would they have to go away and check).

      Outcome:
      3 / 5 users not comfortable using the digital service to apply for Pension Credit, this was mostly due to the length of the form and the fear of completing it incorrectly.
      Users liked being able to see the information in front of them, which was something they didn’t like about the applying on the phone.
      Some of the terminology confused users, household composition, state, P60.
      3 user answered the question ‘Have you lived outside the UK in the last 2 years’ incorrectly, ticking ‘yes’ when they should have selected ‘No’.
      The question of ‘when do you want to claim’ confused users, they were unsure when they could claim from.
      2 user did not realise the next step was to click on the text links, insteading expecting to click on the ‘continue’ button at the bottom of the page.
      2 users expressed concern after reading the declaration. Worrying that if they’d made a mistake they might be prosecuted.

      Next steps:
      Revisit wording on residency ‘Q’
      Reword content such as ‘Household composition’
      Add some content / guidance to help users understand the date they need to enter.
      Test with citizens and their ‘helpers’ as a lot of the users have said they’d normally have seeked help with this type of application
