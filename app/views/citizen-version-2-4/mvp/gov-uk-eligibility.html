Pension Credit

{% include "citizen-version-2-4/mvp/gov-uk-menu.html" %}

Eligibility and entitlement

You can apply for Pension Credit if you live in England, Scotland or Wales and have reached State Pension age.

You can read about Pension Credit in Northern Ireland.

If you live with a partner you can apply for Pension Credit if either:

  • you have both reached State Pension age
  • one of you is getting Housing Benefit for people over State Pension age

A partner is a husband, wife or civil partner or someone else you live with as if you were married.

If anything changes, you should tell us straight away as it can affect how much Pension Credit you get. Read about changes you need to tell us about.

How we work out your income

When you apply for Pension Credit we work out how much you could be entitled to based on your income. This includes:

  • State Pension
  • other pensions
  • earnings from employment and self-employment
  • any other income you have

We will ask you about any money, savings and investments you have. We will not consider the first £10,000 you have. If you have more than £10,000, we will treat every £500 over that amount as an income of £1 a week.

We count most social security benefits as income except for:

  • Attendance Allowance
  • Christmas Bonus
  • Disability Living Allowance
  • Personal Independence Payment
  • Housing Benefit
  • Council Tax Reduction

If you’re entitled to a State Pension, private or workplace pension and have not claimed it yet, we will count the amount you’d expect to get as income from the date you were able to get it.

You will not be able to get the benefit of deferring your State Pension if you or your partner get Pension Credit. You will not build up extra State Pension or a lump sum for deferring your State Pension.
